• Pulao

    Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup Basmati rice
  2. 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, corn, etc.)
  3. 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  4. 1 large tomato, chopped
  5. 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  6. 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  7. 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
    8. 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  8. 4-5 whole peppercorns 4-5 cloves
  9. 2-3 green cardamom pods
  10. 1 black cardamom pod (optional) 1-inch cinnamon stick 1 bay leaf
  11. 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or oil Salt to taste 2 cups water
  12. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

    Recipe:

  1. Wash the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Chop the vegetables into small, uniform-sized pieces.
  3. Heat ghee or oil in a large pan or pot. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add grated ginger, minced garlic, green chilies, and sauté for a minute. Add whole spices - peppercorns, cloves, green cardamom, black cardamom (if using), cinnamon stick, and bay leaf. Sauté for another minute.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate. Add the mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes.
  6. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pan. Stir gently to coat the rice with the spices and vegetables. Pour in the water, add salt, and bring it to a boil. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover with a lid, and simmer until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes.
  7. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it gently with a fork. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.
  8. Vegetable pulao is ready to be served! It pairs well with raita (yogurt with spices), plain yogurt, or a side sala
