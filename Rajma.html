• Rajma

    Ingredients:

  1. Rajma (Red Kidney Beans): 1 cup (soaked overnight or for at least 8 hours)
  2. Onions: 2 large (finely chopped)
  3. Tomatoes: 3 medium-sized (pureed)
  4. Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tablespoon
  5. Green Chilies: 2-3 (slit, adjust to taste)
  6. Cumin Seeds: 1 teaspoon
  7. Coriander Powder: 1 tablespoon
  8. Turmeric Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  9. Red Chili Powder: 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  10. Garam Masala: 1 teaspoon
  11. Salt: To taste
  12. Oil: 2-3 tablespoons
  13. Coriander Leaves: Freshly chopped (for garnish)
  14. Water: As needed

    Recipe:

  1. Soak and Cook Rajma:
  2. Wash and soak the rajma in enough water overnight or for at least 8 hours. Drain and rinse. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked rajma with fresh water. Cook until the rajma is soft and cooked through (usually takes about 15-20 minutes after the first whistle). Set aside. Prepare the Gravy:
  3. In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste and Green Chilies:
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add Tomato Puree:
  5. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala. This might take around 8-10 minutes. Spices:
  6. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook for a couple of minutes, stirring continuously. Combine with Cooked Rajma:
  7. Add the cooked rajma to the masala and mix well. Add salt according to taste. Simmer:
  8. Add water to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer the rajma on low heat for at least 20-30 minutes to let the flavors meld. Mash a Portion of Rajma (Optional):
  9. If you prefer a thicker consistency, you can mash a portion of the rajma with the back of a ladle. Garnish and Serve:
  10. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice or Indian bread like naan or roti.
